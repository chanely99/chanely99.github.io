Staying Sane During a Pandemic

Yup, like everyone and their mother, I too am about to drop some unsolicited advice on how I've been keeping sane during quarantine. Hopefully this'll be helpful, and if not - well, use the googles to find other articles about this exact same topic.

  1. Schedule and commit to your social activities

    2. Being a college student, socializing isn't really too difficult. Okay, making friends is hard at any stage of life, but at school most of them are always within a 10 minute walk from your dorm or apartment.

  2. Use a buddy for accountability

  3. Pick up a "creative" hobby

    4. Yeah so everyone says to pick up a hobby, but in the first few months of quarantine I was still not getting the seratonin I expected from them. I tried listening to podcasts, watching a ton more of Netflix, grinding out leetcode, and reading books from high school, but all of them ended up feeling like chores rather than hobbies.

  4. Get out of the house

    5. Of everything on this list I think this is both the easiest and the most rewarding. Just go outside, walk around the neighborhood and plug in your earbuds. Obviously wear a mask and don't invade the personal space of others. I've walked the same route around my neighborhood at the same time for a consistent 45 minutes every day, and it's done wonders for my mental health.
